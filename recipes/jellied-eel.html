Recipe for Yuca con Mojo

Description

Jellied eels are a traditional British dish that originated in the East End of London. As the name suggests, it consists of eels that have been cooked in a seasoned broth and then left to cool and set, resulting in a jelly-like texture. The eels are typically simmered with ingredients like vinegar, herbs, and spices to create a flavorful stock. Once cooked, the eels are left to cool, allowing the natural gelatin from the eel's skin and bones to solidify the broth into a jelly. The dish is traditionally served cold, with the jellied eels and their accompanying jelly being enjoyed together. Jellied eels have a unique taste and texture that is both rich and slightly gelatinous, making it a distinctive and acquired culinary experience. While once a staple of London's working-class cuisine, jellied eels are now less commonly consumed but still cherished by some as a nostalgic and traditional British delicacy.

Ingredients

    2 pounds (900 grams) eels, cleaned and skinned 2 cups water 1 cup white wine vinegar 1 onion, peeled and roughly chopped 2 cloves garlic, crushed 1 bay leaf 1 teaspoon whole black peppercorns Salt, to taste Lemon wedges and parsley, for garnish (optional)

Steps

    Cut the cleaned and skinned eels into small pieces, around 2-3 inches in length. In a large pot, combine the water, white wine vinegar, chopped onion, crushed garlic, bay leaf, and black peppercorns. Season with salt to taste. Bring the mixture to a boil over medium heat. Once boiling, reduce the heat to low and carefully add the eel pieces to the pot. Simmer the eels gently for about 20-25 minutes, or until they are cooked through and tender. Skim off any impurities or foam that rise to the surface during cooking. Once the eels are cooked, carefully remove them from the cooking liquid using a slotted spoon and set them aside. Discard the bay leaf and peppercorns from the cooking liquid. Pour the remaining cooking liquid into a shallow dish or individual serving containers, ensuring that the eels are fully submerged. Allow the liquid to cool. Once cooled, place the dish or containers in the refrigerator to chill and set for at least 4-6 hours, or overnight. Serve the jellied eels cold, either as a standalone dish or as part of a traditional British meal. Garnish with lemon wedges and parsley, if desired.
